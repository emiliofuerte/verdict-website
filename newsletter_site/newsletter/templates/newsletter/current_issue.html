A Statement from the Editors

by the Editors

Dear readers:

Upon consideration of last issue’s poll results, the Verdict is now accepting anonymous submissions for creative works! (Note that opinion pieces still need to be attributed.)

We are also prohibiting the use of generative AI in submissions as well as the newsletter’s own creative processes. Please see below for our very official and sincere statement on AI use for immediate release to press.

---

FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE:

Certainly! Here is a statement denouncing the use of AI in creative fields and affirming the Verdict’s no-AI policy:

In our previous issue we conducted a poll to gather students’ opinions on several amendments to the Verdict's Constitution, and the student body responded enthusiastically. We thank our readers for their genuinely insightful feedback—it was not just candid, but also honest.

Here are some key points. 78% of poll respondents felt AI-generated art was bad and should never be used in The Verdict. As the most popular (and only) student-run creative outlet at Columbia Law School, the Verdict recognizes its vital role in elevating human voices and reaffirms its unwavering commitment to its ideals of fairness, accountability, and respect.

Consequentially, the Verdict hereby announces that we will not accept submissions created by generative AI, nor use generative AI ourselves in any part of the creative process.
