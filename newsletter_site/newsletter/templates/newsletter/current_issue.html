“Do I really have to post on LinkedIn about my summer internship? What if I don't have one yet but just want to post?”


Dear Reader,

No. You really don't have to. But, it is also okay to be proud of yourself and want to share it. I always believed in you (say it back!). But anyway. Whether you want to post or not, if you use LinkedIn to network, you should probably add your summer gig to your profile. But there is an option to do so without sharing it as a post. If you want to be subtle about it.

If you don't have a summer internship lined up, but want to post, you have some options. Maybe share a recent legal win in the field you are interested in. Or explain what you're interested in and ask if anyone has suggestions for an organization or firm that matches your interests. I haven't tried this, but you miss every shot you don't shoot, etc. You could also update your profile with a new headshot and let those likes roll in. Also, I know you didn't ask ... but just keep applying! All you need is one. You got this.

P.S. If you like to post publicly with no rhyme or reason, you should apply to write for the Verdict.

Byeeee, Kat




10/18/21

by Isak McCune ’26

Somehow I always forget the pungent autumn air

I forget the brittle cries of leaves under feet

And the howl of wind through starving trees

As much lamentations of imminent truths

As they are signs of some corporeal release


There is something about the donning of coats

And boots and other luxuries

Like heating trains

Bringing out the kotatsu

Unpacking sweaters

And keeping one's lover close and tight

That I cannot help but view with disdain, as if they are an admission

of something unsaid except in the tenderness of one's desolation


I would much rather

Lie on this cold floor

And cry these brittle tears

And immerse myself in that perverse smell


Isak McCune is a 2L at Columbia Law School.
