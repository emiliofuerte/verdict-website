A Message to the Social Justice Crowd: Less Preachiness, More Anti-Poverty

by Daniel Bugingo ’26

“There’s not quite an anti-poverty club at CLS. The organizations that come to my mind (LPE, NLG, FGP, various arms of various affinity groups) don’t fit the bill since they’re more geared toward professional networking, shared identity, or shared ideology than poverty. It’s good and fine and normal to network professionally and hang out with whatever feels like one’s crowd—this is an expensive education and a lonely city. But I can’t stop thinking about this David Brooks line from last fall: ‘As the left veered toward identitarian performance art, Donald Trump jumped into the class war with both feet.’”
